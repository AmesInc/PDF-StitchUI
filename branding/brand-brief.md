# PDF-StitchUI Brand Brief

## Brand Position
PDF-StitchUI is a practical desktop tool for assembling and splitting PDFs with a visual, tile-based workflow. The identity should feel dependable, precise, and desktop-native rather than playful or generic SaaS.

## Core Brand Ideas
- Document-first: clearly reads as a PDF/document tool at a glance
- Stitch action: communicates combining, joining, sequencing, or binding
- Structured workflow: hints at tiles, order, or page allocation
- Native utility: feels like a serious productivity app, not a consumer toy

## Visual Principles
- Avoid copying the Adobe PDF logo directly
- Keep the shape simple enough to read at small app-icon sizes
- Prefer bold silhouette over intricate line detail
- Use a strong central metaphor: seam, join, interlock, or ordered stack
- Make Stitch the memorable part of the identity

## Recommended Color Direction
- Primary red: deep document red, not bright candy red
- Support charcoal: for structure, outlines, and desktop-tool weight
- Optional accent blue: for active UI states, selection, or split-mode contrast
- Backgrounds should support both light and neutral system surfaces

## Recommended Typography Tone
- Solid, technical, readable
- Slightly industrial or editorial rather than rounded/playful
- Wordmark emphasis should visually favor Stitch inside PDF-StitchUI

## Direction 1: Document Seam
Concept:
- A single page or two overlapped pages with a visible stitched seam or bound join running vertically or diagonally.

Why it works:
- Best small-size legibility
- Directly communicates stitching/assembly
- Strong fit for app icon and installer branding

Prompt:
Create a logo concept sheet for a desktop PDF tool named PDF-StitchUI. Show a clean, vector-style app icon and small wordmark for the concept "Document Seam." Use a document/page silhouette with a folded corner, but do not copy the Adobe PDF logo. Add a bold stitched seam or bound join through the page to represent combining documents. Style should feel precise, modern, desktop-native, and productivity-focused. Use a restrained palette: deep PDF red, charcoal, and subtle steel-gray. Present the concept on a clean light background with icon, wordmark, and a few small-size app icon previews.

## Direction 2: Linked Pages
Concept:
- Two document shapes or corners interlock like links, implying connection and merge.

Why it works:
- Strong metaphor for joining files
- Slightly more dynamic and brandable
- Good for repo avatar and release art

Prompt:
Create a logo concept sheet for a desktop PDF tool named PDF-StitchUI. Show a clean, vector-style app icon and small wordmark for the concept "Linked Pages." Use two document/page shapes with folded corners that interlock like linked parts, suggesting merging or joining PDFs. Do not imitate Adobe branding. Style should be bold, geometric, and professional, with strong small-size clarity. Use deep document red, charcoal, and a subtle cool-gray accent. Present the concept on a clean light background with icon, wordmark, and several favicon or app-tile scale previews.

## Direction 3: Tile Sequence
Concept:
- Several page or tile forms arranged in sequence and resolving into one final document.

Why it works:
- Best match to the actual stitch UI
- Connects branding to drag-and-drop tile behavior
- Useful for product screenshots and onboarding visuals

Prompt:
Create a logo concept sheet for a desktop PDF tool named PDF-StitchUI. Show a clean, vector-style app icon and small wordmark for the concept "Tile Sequence." Use 3 to 4 ordered document tiles or page cards flowing into one final assembled page, suggesting sequence and stitching. Keep it simple and app-icon friendly, not busy. Style should feel like a serious desktop productivity tool, with crisp geometry and subtle motion implied through arrangement. Use deep PDF red, charcoal, and a muted steel-blue accent on a clean light background. Include icon, wordmark, and small-size preview variations.
